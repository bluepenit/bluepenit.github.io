How it works

Purrweb has a pool of developers, designers, testers, DevOps, analysts, and managers. With oustaffing, you can choose any specialist from that pool and hire them to work on your project. They will effectively become part of your in-house team, focusing only on the tasks that you give them.

Outstaffing ≠ outsourcing
When you outsource, you delegate a task to another team and pay for the result. This is a good option if you don’t want to be too involved in the process. When you outstaff, however, you hire specialists to work in your team and pay for their skills and expertise — this means that you’re fully involved in all the processes.
With outstaffing, you…
  • Pay for the time, skills & expertise
  • Manage all the processes
  • Get to pick the specialists
With outsourcing, you…
  • Pay for the result
  • Give feedback with no direct control
  • Hire a pre-made team

FAQ s

  • What specialists do you outstaff?

    Our pool of 200 specialists covers all the key roles in IT & app development. We outstaff developers, designers, testers, analysts, and managers. There are no juniors among our candidates: only middles, seniors, and architects. All of them have worked on multiple projects in various niches and gained substantial expertise in their respective fields.

  • Who needs IT outstaffing services?

    Most companies that work in IT can benefit from the services of an outstaffing company — regardless of their size and segment. Outstaffing is a convenient model that will help you replace absent team members, scale your project, or simply make the development process a bit easier when you’re forced to work under heavy load.

  • What’s so good about outstaffing?

    First and foremost, you get to directly manage the personnel that you hire: you can set deadlines, delegate tasks, and pretty much fully control the direction in which they’re going. You’ll be working with them like you usually work with an in-house team. You also don’t have to waste time on recruiting, paperwork, and other HR processes — just pick the right specialists, and they will dive headfirst into the development process.

  • What’s the difference between outstaffing and outsourcing?

    When you choose software development outsourcing, you delegate a project to another team and pay only for the result. For instance, a founder wants to redesign their app, and they ask a design agency to do it for them. Outstaffing works differently: instead of delegating the project, you hire skilled professionals and manage them directly. In this case, you pay for their time and experience, and you can achieve the desired result in any way you deem necessary — unlike software development outsourcing, when you have no control over the processes in another company.
